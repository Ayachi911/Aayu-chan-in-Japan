Yokohama, Japan — 2021

I started writing young — first published at ten, which sounds more impressive than it probably was. What it actually meant was that I understood, early on, that the act of writing is also the act of paying attention. You notice things differently when you know you might later have to explain them to someone who wasn't there.

In 2021, I was selected as an Asia Kakehashi Project scholar and spent ten months living with a host family and attending Hiranuma High School in Yokohama, Japan. The scholarship is a cultural exchange initiative run between Japan and various Asian countries, and it puts students in the middle of ordinary Japanese life — not tourist Japan, not curated-experience Japan, but the version with early train commutes, bento boxes, kyudo practice after class, and host family dinners on weekday evenings.

It was one of the more clarifying years of my life. Not because Japan is exotic — it isn't, once you've been there long enough — but because being genuinely outside your own cultural frame for an extended period teaches you things about yourself that you can't learn any other way. The things you assumed were universal. The social instincts you didn't know you had. The moments where you realize silence means something completely different here.

What this publication is

This is not a travel blog. I'm not interested in itineraries or "top ten things to do in Tokyo" content. What I've written here are guides and observations for a specific audience: students considering exchange programs, people preparing for Japan, and anyone curious about what the day-to-day of a year abroad actually looks like when the Instagram posts stop and the real logistics begin.

The guides are practical. The essays are honest. Nothing here is exaggerated for effect, and nothing is softened to be more palatable. If something was confusing or uncomfortable or genuinely difficult, I've written it that way.

I'm interested in culture, language, international institutions, and the strange experience of belonging somewhere temporarily. This publication is where I document those interests — through guides, through observation, and occasionally through longer essays when a topic demands it.

A note on accuracy

Exchange programs change. Prices change. Policies change. I've noted where information may have shifted since my year and will continue updating guides as I learn of changes. If something here is outdated, please reach out — I'd rather correct it than leave bad information circulating.
