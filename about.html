About

Ayachi
Samyal

BTech Student · Published Author · Asia Kakehashi Scholar · ML Researcher

I am a BTech student based in Chandigarh, India, currently studying at Amity University. I have been publishing work — comics, poetry, writing — since I was ten years old. The first one was a comic about a character named Sam Saira. The second sold over two hundred copies and was donated to the Kailash Satyarthi Foundation. I was the youngest speaker at the Sangrur Literary Festival that year.

In 2021, I was selected by the Japanese government for the Asia Kakehashi Project — a fully-funded year-long cultural ambassadorship in Japan. I was one of twenty-one students selected from India, and about two hundred and fifty across Asia. I was sixteen. I lived with a host family in Yokohama, attended Hiranuma High School full-time, studied Kyuudo, practiced tea ceremony, and took every exam in Japanese. I scored second highest in the school-wide English examination among all students.

That year is the subject of most of this writing. The essays on this site use it as a lens for thinking about soft power, cultural exchange, and international relations. The guides are written for high schoolers who want to do what I did — with everything I wish someone had told me.

Outside writing: I do ML research. My first internship was a water quality prediction project at C-DAC Mohali, a government research institution. I am currently working on diatom-based fuel analysis under Dr. Sarika Grover in Environmental Sciences. The long-term direction is the intersection of machine learning and international policy — using computational methods to study the things I write about.

I also run two Pinterest accounts with a combined monthly reach of 400,000+. One is illustration. One is photography and aesthetics. I built both from zero, which taught me more about content strategy and algorithmic thinking than most courses do. I speak five languages. I draw traditionally with ink pens, and I am currently working on an original manga series.

If you want to get in touch — for commissions, collaborations, research, or otherwise — email is best: samyalayachi@gmail.com.
